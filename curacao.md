# Curacao

Breezy sparely populated caribbean island and Dutch colony.

It is expensive and the demographic here tends to be the older crowd.

It is small but not small enough to walk around and the taxis will cost you a lot.

Even in Willemsted, the capital, the weeds have reclaimed some pavements,
and some buildings are derelict with plants growing through them.

This place clearly survives on tourism and not much else.

## Travel in Curacao

Taxis are expensive

## Willemstad

The capital, sparse with a few gentrified roads, mainly centred around Punda, the historic centre,
and the Queen Emma moving bridge connecting Otrobanda.

### Willemsted Airport

Small airport.

Taxi from the airport cost a stagginer \$38 for 20 minutes,
I don't remember the last time I paid that much for a taxi except for London.

### Best Areas in Willemstad

- [Punda](https://maps.app.goo.gl/JuZS2y2zViuEByw6A) - historic center of Willemstad with colourful Dutch colonial
  buildings, shops and waterfront cafes
- [Otrobanda](https://maps.app.goo.gl/HZjZj5imqXSRRScP8) - vibrant cultural district with street art, museums and views
  across St Anna Bay
- [Pietermaai](https://maps.app.goo.gl/btAihbgFM2916ZcT6) - trendy seaside quarter known for boutique hotels,
  restaurants and nightlife
- [Jan Thiel](https://maps.app.goo.gl/qt9U33GW26LYW8kg6) - popular beach area with resorts, water sports and a lively
  coastal atmosphere
- [Blue Bay](https://maps.app.goo.gl/F5NdhXFjRrcduuWdA) - gated resort community with golf course, private beach and
  modern villas
- [Julianadorp](https://maps.app.goo.gl/mo7MJByaf7X9GPAH7) - quiet residential neighbourhood with leafy streets popular
  with families and expats
- [Mahaai](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mahaai+Willemstad+Curacao) - central residential area close
  to schools, supermarkets and daily amenities
- [Brakkeput](https://maps.app.goo.gl/XebN1Z6Y2ijub5Mr8) - peaceful hillside and waterfront area
  near Spanish Water with spacious homes

### Attractions in Punda / Otrobanda

- [Queen Emma Bridge](https://maps.app.goo.gl/accwZxqxYSDggGE26) - floating pontoon bridge that moves to the side to let
  cruise ships pass,
  meaning you could have to wait half an hour to re-cross as the alternative route is a very high car bridge
- [Curaçao Sign](https://maps.app.goo.gl/cvS6LcztmUi2TsLF6)
- [Punda Beach Curaçao "Playa Marichi"](https://maps.app.goo.gl/FY2cTbc2RCY1vbL88)

### Restaurants in Punda / Otrobanda

- [Grand Café 'Gouverneur De Rouville' Curaçao](https://maps.app.goo.gl/yBwctM1tf1bivv5d8) - a nearly 300 years old,
  this place is a treat but you will need to book a table in advance for the lovely balcony.
  The Cuban banana soup was good, but not spicy.
  The mixed grill of small beef tenderloin steak,
  caribbean chicken and 4 shrimp skewer (100g for each of the three types) comes with a small fries and mayo.
  The portions are small enough to leave space for dessert, a bit surprising for the price.
  The homemade apple pie with vanilla ice cream is ok but not sure it's worth 18 XCG.
  WiFi on the terrace was a bit unrealiable so no speed test succeeded
- [La Bohème Curaçao](https://maps.app.goo.gl/swCDFKELm9pXzW73A) - popular restaurant in Punda.
  The ceviche had sold out so tried the Karni Stoba traditional beef stew but that was surprisingly mediocre,
  in both portion and flavour, especially for 20 XCG ($10).
  Next time get the ceviche or the lomo saltado and hope its half as good as the peruvians make it
