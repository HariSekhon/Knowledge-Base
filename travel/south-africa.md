## Summary

[South Africa](https://maps.app.goo.gl/yEA5t4pci6E3PN7HA) is one of the most dangerous countries in the world.

I would not take any white girlfriend there (or to most of Africa).

It was a 1st world developed country with good infrastructure built by European settlers which is now deteriorating
under democratic native rule.

See [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uxEWUgqjscw) for before and after screenshots showing this.

There are now regular electricity blackouts due to failure to maintain the working electric grid they inherited.
Interestingly this also happened in the [Dominican Republic](dominican-republic.md) the first night I stayed there.
The common factor is the population.

South Africa has high inequality - the Gini coefficient of inequality correlates to crime -
except not in East Asia, disproving poverty as the cause of crime - it is the natives.

You will need a car in South Africa as its road infrastructure and distancing is similar to the US.

Make sure to lock your doors immediately to reduce risk of carjacking and beware are red light stops of beggars and
dangerous natives.

## Crime

South Africa is one of the most dangerous countries in the world with horrible crimes especially against non-blacks
being well known for at least 20 years, but it has continued to deteriorate under native rule to the point where even
Zimbabwean black immigrants are now being killed.

For context, Zimbabwe is even less productive after expelling white farmers so
Zimbabweans move to South Africa for jobs and the South African blacks resent them for this.

Houses have bars on their windows, barred gates with 2 dogs, and guns for good reason.

Violent home invasions are a common problem -
one of my white South African colleagues in the UK emigrated from South Africa after a home invasion
where a gang tore off his gate using chains attached to a truck and stormed it.

If he and his family had left home a mere 30 minutes later it would have been a horror upon them.

You may only get one close call like that in life.
