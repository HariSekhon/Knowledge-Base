## Montevideo

The capital. You don't need more than 2-3 days as it's not that big.

Montevideo is 2-3 degrees colder than Buenos Aires across the water and also has more wind chill,
making it feel several degrees colder than that,
likely due to it being more exposed to the open ocean compared to Buenos Aires which is set back from the ocean front.

### Travel from Buquebus Ferry

If coming from [Buenos Aires](argentina.md) via the ferry (1/3rd the price of plane) arriving
at [Terminal de Buquebus Montevideo], you can do one of the following:

- Taxi App - Uber quoted roughly $U 500 (\$13)
- Take a bus for $U 64 (\$1.70) quite easily to your final destination, consult Google Maps

### Taxi Apps in Montevideo

- [Uber](https://www.uber.com/uy/en/) - moderately priced, has a few cars on the map
- [InDrive](https://indrive.com/) - I didn't see cars on the map for this so it might not work

### Buses in Montevideo

Several bus lines go between east and west of the city making it easy to get around cheaply,
and the city is not that big anyway, just over a 1 hour walk if you wanted to.

Bus trips cost $U 52-64 (\$1.70) as of Feb 2026.

You need to get an STM prepaid travel card, which you can luckily pick up at one of these common convenience stores:

- [Abitab](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Abitab/) - they insisted on a national bank card for card payment or
  Uruguyuan Pesos cash - luckily there was also a Cambio money changer there to convert some dollars or euros
- [Redpagos](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Redpagos/)

The card itself is free although requires your passport for them to register the card to you,
and then you just top up some cash on the card.

I've found Google Maps planning of public transport route to be reliable here, even down to bus stops and arrival times.
