## Summary

[Paraguay](https://maps.app.goo.gl/AXC3CMD7mjavrRYf9) is a landlocked 3rd world country in between Argentina and Brazil
that offers easy residency and tax advantages.

The main complaint is that it's boring and there isn't much to do there -
which I can attest to - I wouldn't want to live there.

## Population

[Paraguay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paraguay) has nearly 7 million people - mostly mestizo - a mix of indigenous
Guaraní Native American and Spanish.

Its birthrate is still above the replenishment figure of 2.1 children per woman unlike the declining Western
democracies, although it's birthrate is declining so expected to go below replenishment within a few years.

## Asuncion

The capital - I found it boring almost immediately and have no plans to revisit.

### Asuncion Airport - Silvio Pettirossi International

There is surprisingly little in this airport,
after security you have the usual duty free shop and 2 cafes - both chains - a Juan Valdez and Havanna.

There is a small section of craft boutiques around the corner from the duty free, half of which were closed at 7am when
I was departing.

The Wifi is open without captcha portal as of March 2026
but it's so slow and intermittent as to be unusable with web pages not loading and speed test doesn't work at all
(signal strength was fine and basic pings pass - but only intermittently - it's a traffic problem).

### Travel within Asuncion

There is no underground metro train system like in developed countries.

The options are either

1. Buses call _"colectivos"_
2. Taxis

#### Buses in Asuncion

Buses in Asuncion need a prepaid card instead of cash.

I walked around to several places including supermarkets and pharmacies that locals told me I could buy one.

After an hour of walking around in the afternoon heat of 36 degrees, I got frustrated and just took an Uber.

#### Taxis in Asuncion

- [Uber](https://www.uber.com/py/en/) - there are relatively few taxis so wait times can be long.
  The motorbike Uber is cheaper but a longer wait, and I had to hold on for dear life as the driver went full speed over
  potholes, braked hard at traffic lights and ran the last red light before my destination!
  While motorbikes are fun, it might be safer to take a regular car Uber here
- ~~[InDrive](https://indrive.com/en-py)~~ - there were no InDrive drivers on the map or offers to my bid in Asuncion

### Restaurants in Asuncion

- [Bolsi](https://maps.app.goo.gl/fdCpmfyfdhPV4R7c8) - popular local restaurant with a wide menu range. The bife de chorizo steak (strip steak, not chorizo)
  was an inch thick and juicy medium rare,
  with smashed rustic pototo and shoestring onion rings - this was excellent for 115,000 PYG ($18).
  The hot volcano chocolate cake with vanilla ice cream was also very good.
  Their fresh natural juices are only \$2.50.
  There is both indoor and outdoor seating, but there was a queue 5 tables deep for the outdoor seating.
  The indoor seating is an American style diner ring table around the restaurant, with sports on the TV screens.
  Wifi 0.6/1 Mbit/s
- [El Café de Acá](https://maps.app.goo.gl/47GUJETVQfCSTW2w6) - gentrified cafe restaurant with good menu options
  ranging from beef and chicken to desserts on cabinet display, and reasonable prices too for such an upmarket area.
  It has a small front garden seating, very nice internal decor, air conditioned with different seating areas depending on whether you
  want coffee shop style or restaurant style seating, and mid sized back garden with
  seating and low romantic evening lighting.
  The lomito callejero in brioche with a side of fries was decent, especially for the good price,
  although the beef steak was thinner than I would have liked compared to the volume of bread,
  but it had an egg in there too to prop up the protein.
  The fries were crispy and decent and they have fresh orange juice too.
  Has wifi
- [Bellini Palma](https://maps.app.goo.gl/JWzXxqWVJrfT46Nt5) - good quality and menu, nice interior seating booths -
  moderately upmarket priced - it adds up!
  The steak with garlic on it was substantial
  (the house recommendation is the one without the garlic) which comes with a light salad,
  so ensure to add a side dish.
  The gratinated potatoes in cream with melted cheese on top was very good too.
  The brownie was ice cream had a much bigger large triangle shaped brownie than expected,
  compared to the usual small square brownie many places do for this common dessert.
  The brownie was so big that the large ice cream scoop was set on top of it.
  They also have fresh juices (orange / pineapple / peach) -
  the orange was better and slightly bigger than the pineapple one.
  Wifi 107/24 Mbit/s
- [Lido Bar](https://maps.app.goo.gl/FUML8xYD2avH5qGi6) - established in 1953 this diner style restaurant is popular and
  mid priced.
  The staff didn't speak English however and brought me a lomo
  (beef)
  milanese
  when I asked for a lomo marinara which if you Google it is supposed to be in tomato sauce not deep fried like a
  milanese.
  I generally wouldn't recommend ordering this due to inflammatory unhealthy nature of it,
  but it was at least a large size and fills the plate by itself (you need to order sides separately),
  but the beef steak is very thin, so it's probably mostly batter by volume.
  I would order something else next time.
  They do have freshly squeezed juice.
  The main Lido Bar is opposite the Metropolitan Cathedral of Our Lady of the Assumption, but there is
  [another one](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Euq6uD4RfDkxYEW67) in between the more upmarket Recoleta and Villa Mora areas.
  Has wifi
- [Açaiterra](https://maps.app.goo.gl/LgFVG7H742537ar46) - acai chain, there is one at the
  [Mariscal](https://maps.app.goo.gl/jGmHTkJodQ8eFTN97) mall too.
  Their medium sized cup is good value at 31,000 PYG ($4.80) by international standards.
  I had the Tropical which was mostly fruit toppings including strawberry, banana, pineapple, kiwi, coconut flakes,
  peanut butter and it hit the spot

### Areas Yet to Explore

- [San Bernardino](https://maps.app.goo.gl/qgwrANi9LFmkArbs7) – popular lakeside resort town for day trips and
  relaxation.
  Although this looks nearby, only 1.5 hours on the main stretch (plus taxis or buses on either side),
  getting there is actually quite a hassle.
  The last few miles to San Benedino no buses came for an hour and neither did the Ubers,
  even at priority level, or motorbikes.
  I spent 4 hours trying to get there with a combination of Uber taxis long distance buses and before giving up and
  going back to Asuncion before getting stranded if no return bus or taxis were available given the unreliability of
  both on that last stretch.
  The [motorway toll booth](https://maps.app.goo.gl/izg9h6DCGxZzdS8N6) in Ypacaraí
  also causes lots traffic on both sides and traffic on the way back to Asuncion was bad even after that too,
  I don't know why, wasting over 6 hours in total in the attempt.
  Note to self, don't try this again on Heroes Day, their national holiday.
  Next time rent a car for the day
- [Ciudad del Este](https://maps.app.goo.gl/m9tJGR1z4VX8p91b7) - border city with shopping and budget stays
