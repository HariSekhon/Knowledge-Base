## Belmopan

The capital, but lacks attractions, do not stay here, it is only useful as a transit to the other locations.

Not considered particularly safe.

## San Ignacio (Cayo District) – Best for Adventure & Culture

Great base for exploring Mayan ruins (eg. Xunantunich, Caracol) and caves (eg. ATM Cave).

Surrounded by rainforest, rivers, and nature reserves.

Popular with backpackers, eco-travelers, and those wanting to see inland Belize.

## Ambergris Caye (San Pedro) – Best for Beaches & Diving

Most popular tourist destination in Belize.

Great access to the Barrier Reef, snorkeling, and diving (eg. Hol Chan Marine Reserve, Blue Hole).

Wide range of accommodations, from hostels to luxury resorts.

Lively atmosphere with restaurants, bars, and beach clubs.

## Caye Caulker – Best for Laid-back Island Life

Smaller, quieter, and cheaper than Ambergris Caye.

Perfect for backpackers and travelers who prefer a slow, chill pace (“Go Slow” is literally the motto).

Easy access to snorkeling and diving trips.

No cars—just golf carts and bicycles.

## Placencia – Best for Beaches & Relaxation

On the mainland, with a beautiful long peninsula and calm beaches.

Good mix of local culture and small resorts.

Ideal for couples or those who want beaches without the crowds of San Pedro.

Access to reef and jungle tours.
